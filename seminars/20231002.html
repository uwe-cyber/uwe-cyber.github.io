UWEcyber Seminar Series 2023-24

Cyber Path - Police & Academia Talent Horizons

Mark Moore, South West Cyber Resilience Centre

Monday 2nd October, 12:00, Room 4Q56, Frenchay Campus, UWE Bristol
Abstract

The South West Cyber Resilience Centre is a police-private partnership which supports over 1300 regional small businesses and charities in becoming more cyber-resilient. As part of its work, it enables regional students to sharpen their skills in a business environment, testing systems and delivering plans to support organisational resilience. SWCRC is undergoing its annual recruitment round, offering an opportunity for training, networking, and CV building, undertaking paid work at a time to suit you. This presentation will give you the opportunity to find out more, and ask questions.

Bio

Mark is a serving police Superintendent in the Devon and Cornwall force, and has been CEO of the South West Cyber Resilience Centre for three years. After graduating from Exeter University with a languages degree, he has worked for 28 years in policing, spanning a variety of operational roles. Most recently, his time with the cyber resilience centre has led him to develop extensive contacts within the regional cyber landscape, supporting well over a thousand small business and charities through a partnership approach. Mark has operated nationally as a lead consultant to policing, helping forces across the country create sustainable operating model, and chaired the board of directors of a local Multi-Academy Trust, guiding it through a period of restructure and expansion. Mark holds an MSc in police leadership and management and a postgraduate diploma in the psychology of organisation development. He is married with two children, two dogs, and a passion for Cornish beaches.

UWEcyber host a weekly seminar series, on campus, at the University of the West of England (UWE Bristol). It is an opportunity to hear from invited speakers from industry, academia and government on all things related to cyber security, to expand your knowledge and understand across the broad spectrum of cyber security. All are welcome to attend our seminars, specifically all postgraduate and undergraduate students studying cyber security at UWE. We encourage you to attend, listen, discuss and debate - speakers are quite often keen to talk about job opportunities - so another excellent reason to join and take part in the discussions!
